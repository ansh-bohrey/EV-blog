# RS‑485 / RS‑232 — Serial Links That Still Matter

*Prerequisites: [Communication Interface →](./communication-interface.md)*

---

## Serial Before CAN

RS‑232 and RS‑485 predate CAN but remain common in chargers, battery racks, and BMS debug ports. They carry UART data and define the electrical layer that makes UART work over cables.

---

## UART Basics (Shared Foundation)

UART frames use a start bit, 8 data bits, optional parity, and stop bits. There is no shared clock, so both ends must agree on the baud rate.

Common baud rates: 9600, 19200, 57600, 115200.

---

## RS‑232 — Single‑Ended, Short‑Range

- Logic levels: +3 to +15 V for 0, ‑3 to ‑15 V for 1.
- Single‑ended relative to ground.
- Point‑to‑point only.
- Practical distance: short.

Typical uses in BMS work:

- Debug UART output
- Bootloader firmware updates
- Legacy charger configuration

---

## Why RS‑485 Was Needed

RS‑232 is fragile in noisy environments and cannot support multi‑drop networks. RS‑485 solves both with differential signaling and a bus topology.

---

## RS‑485 — Differential, Long‑Range

- Differential signaling on A/B lines.
- Multi‑drop bus (32+ nodes, more with high‑impedance receivers).
- Up to ~1200 m at low speed.
- Half‑duplex on 2‑wire, full‑duplex on 4‑wire.

Typical BMS uses:

- Module polling in battery racks
- Charger communication
- EMS or energy storage interfaces

---

## Termination and Biasing

- 120 Ohm terminations at both ends of the bus.
- Bias resistors define idle state when all drivers are tri‑stated.
- Missing biasing causes random characters on an idle bus.

---

## Direction Control (Half‑Duplex)

Most RS‑485 designs are 2‑wire. The transceiver has DE/RE pins:

- Assert DE to transmit.
- De‑assert DE to listen.

Poor DE timing is the most common RS‑485 bug.

---

## Modbus RTU (Common Protocol on RS‑485)

RS‑485 is only the physical layer. Modbus RTU is the most common protocol on top.

Frame structure:

- Address (1 byte)
- Function code (1 byte)
- Data (variable)
- CRC‑16 (2 bytes)

Typical BMS register map:

- 0x0000: SOC x10
- 0x0001: Pack voltage x10
- 0x0002: Pack current x10
- 0x0003: Fault bitmask

---

## RS‑232 vs RS‑485 vs CAN (Quick Compare)

| Property | RS‑232 | RS‑485 | CAN |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signaling | Single‑ended | Differential | Differential |
| Nodes | 2 | 32+ | 110 |
| Distance | Short | Long | Medium |
| Collision handling | None | Master‑slave | Arbitration |
| Typical BMS use | Debug | Rack comms | Vehicle bus |

---

## Takeaways

- RS‑232 is simple and ideal for debug ports.
- RS‑485 is robust for long‑distance multi‑drop links.
- Modbus RTU makes RS‑485 practical for battery racks and chargers.
