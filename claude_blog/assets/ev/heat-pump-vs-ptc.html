Heat Pump vs PTC Heater — Range Impact

COP (Coefficient of Performance) determines how much battery energy is consumed for cabin heating

Ambient temperature (°C) −5°C
Heat demand (kW) 4.0 kW
Nominal range (km) 400 km
PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient) Simple resistive heater — like a kettle
COP = 1.0 always (100% efficiency)
4 kW heat = 4 kW drawn from battery
Cheap, reliable, works at any temperature
Used in: older Leaf, early EVs
→ Major range penalty in cold weather
Heat Pump (Refrigeration cycle reversed) Moves heat from outside air → cabin
COP = 2–4 at mild temperatures
4 kW heat ≈ 1–2 kW drawn from battery
COP drops as temp falls (less heat to extract)
Below −15°C: often falls back to PTC assist
Used in: Tesla Model Y (2021+), VW ID.4, Hyundai Ioniq 5
→ Significantly better winter range
BMS role in cabin heating BMS monitors battery temperature
If battery <15°C, must warm cells before charge
Coolant loop: heater ↔ battery ↔ cabin
Battery self-heating from current also helps
Pre-conditioning: heat battery + cabin while plugged in
→ Pre-conditioning while plugged = free range
