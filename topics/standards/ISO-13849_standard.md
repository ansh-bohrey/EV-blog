# ISO 13849 — Blog Plan

## Goal
Explain ISO 13849 for safety‑related control systems, how it differs from ISO 26262, and when it matters for EV or battery systems.

## Audience Angles
- **Engineers / students**: Performance Level (PL), categories, MTTFd, diagnostic coverage.
- **EV enthusiasts**: Why different safety standards exist and how they affect system design.

---

## Subtopic Flow

### 1. Hook — Why Another Safety Standard?
- ISO 26262 is for road vehicles.
- ISO 13849 is for machinery and industrial systems.
- Battery systems cross domains (EVs, storage, equipment).

### 2. Scope and Applicability
- Machinery vs automotive contexts.
- When ISO 13849 appears in energy storage and charging equipment.

### 3. Key Concepts
- Performance Level (PL a–e).
- Categories (B, 1, 2, 3, 4).
- MTTFd and Diagnostic Coverage.

### 4. Comparing ISO 13849 and ISO 26262
- Process‑based vs performance‑level focus.
- Risk assessment approach.
- Documentation expectations.

### 5. Example Mapping to BMS Functions
- Safe shutdown circuit as a safety‑related control function.
- HV interlock loop as a protective function.
- Contactor diagnostics as a safety mechanism.

### 6. Takeaways
- ISO 13849 is relevant for non‑automotive battery systems.
- Engineers must identify the right safety framework for the application.
